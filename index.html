Research Statement

My doctoral research sits at the intersection of sports science, neuroscience, and data science. I use neurophysiological measurements — primarily surface electromyography (sEMG) — to objectively quantify muscle function in athletes with physical impairments. A central focus is understanding how volitional effort can be distinguished from maximal effort during Para sport classification assessments.

The integrity of Paralympic classification depends on accurate, reproducible, and tamper-resistant measurements. By combining high-density EMG with machine-learning-based classifiers, I aim to create tools that help classifiers make evidence-based decisions and protect fair competition.
