Project 1

Variable Force Matching Protocol

The core of the PhD: a novel experimental paradigm where participants track dynamically varying force targets during isometric contractions. Surface EMG patterns and force-tracking error are used to distinguish genuine neuromuscular limitation from intentional effort reduction.

This protocol directly addresses the problem of boosting — deliberately performing below capacity during classification to gain a competitive advantage.

Project 2

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation Assessment

Using TMS and motor nerve stimulation to directly interrogate the corticomotor pathway during isometric effort. Voluntary activation assessment via twitch interpolation enables objective quantification of neural drive to muscle.

Comparing TMS-derived measures in genuine maximum contractions vs. submaximal efforts to establish discriminant benchmarks for classification contexts.

Project 3

Statistical Modelling & Algorithm Development

Five custom MATLAB algorithms developed for real-time experimental control, automated EMG signal processing, artefact rejection, and statistical analysis pipelines — improving data handling efficiency by 60%.

Advanced statistical modelling in R and Python, including regression, multivariate analysis, and machine learning approaches for classification signal discrimination.
